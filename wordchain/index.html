Every word is a point in 100-dimensional space, and two words are linked by the cosine of the angle between them. The board is a complete weighted graph — but a link is only drawn when it's stronger than your current weakest link, which is why the two halves stay apart until you've genuinely bridged them.

Finding your best route is the widest path problem: Dijkstra's, but maximizing the minimum edge instead of summing them. That bottleneck is your score. Layout is a hand-rolled force simulation — springs on the links, repulsion everywhere else.

Vectors are GloVe, mean-centered and quantized to int8 by build_embeddings.py. No server, no libraries — 1.2 MB of int8 and a <canvas>.
